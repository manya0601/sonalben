Our Scrapbook

A Family Built on Crunch

Discover our family album and the steps that shaped Sonalben Khakhrawala

Wooden rolling pin, 1990
First Batch!
1990

A Small Home Kitchen

Sonalben started in her cozy home kitchen, rolling thin whole wheat discs on a simple wooden board. Roasting them over a slow flame, she served neighbors who immediately loved the clean, crispy texture.

Iron pan roasting
1993

First Handmade Batches

As orders grew, the family came together to stone-grind local spices like cumin and fenugreek. Pressing and roasting each batch by hand on heavy iron griddles to maintain the homemade goodness.

Bicycle deliveries
Pure Wheat 🌾
1997

Early Local Customers

Neighborhood tea stalls and local stores began ordering daily stacks. The family packed the fresh snacks in simple craft bags, delivering them on bicycles. Trust grew, one daily bite at a time.

First Store, Ahmedabad
2005

Growing Trust in Ahmedabad

We raised our first commercial retail awning in Ahmedabad. The city embraced Sonalben's fresh, hand-roasted khakhras as the ultimate companion to hot afternoon ginger tea.

Traditional expansion
2012

Opening Dedicated Stores

We expanded to dedicated branches across different sectors of the city. We added fresh, crispy Fafda, melt-in-mouth Gathiya, and spicy Sev, staying true to our core family kitchen standards.

Celebrating family
For Bappa 🪔
2018

Serving Generations of Families

Sonalben's snacks became a staple of daily life, marriage hampers, and school recesses. Children who once ate our bicycle snacks now bring their own kids to our storefronts.

Ahmedabad, Today
Today

Modern Sonalben Today

While Sonalben now exports domestic and international packages, our core process remains unchanged. Every khakhra is still hand-pressed, roasted, and packed with the same family warmth we started with in 1990.

Family Cookbook

Our Kitchen Secrets

Traditional snacks and delicious fusions you can easily make at home

Uses: Fresh Thepla ⏱️ 15 Mins

Pizza Thepla Wrap

Roll a classic fenugreek-spiced thepla into a delicious wrap filled with capsicum, paneer, oregano, and cream cheese.

Key Ingredients: Thepla, pizza sauce, paneer, capsicum, liquid cheese.
  1. Heat the thepla on a flat pan with a dab of butter.
  2. Spread 1 tablespoon of pizza sauce evenly down the center.
  3. Add capsicum strips and lightly sautéed paneer cubes.
  4. Drizzle melted cheese and sprinkle pizza spices. Roll tight and serve.
Uses: Masala Khakhra ⏱️ 5 Mins

Masala Khakhra Chaat

A crunchy, light street chat. Roughly crushed masala khakhra topped with potatoes, onions, tamarind chutney, and sev.

Key Ingredients: Masala Khakhra, boiled potato, chopped onion, sweet chutney, fine sev.
  1. Lightly crush 2 Masala Khakhras into a serving plate.
  2. Layer with cubes of boiled potatoes and chopped raw onions.
  3. Drizzle sweet date-tamarind chutney and spicy green chutney.
  4. Garnish heavily with fine gram flour sev and fresh coriander. Eat immediately.
Uses: Plain Khakhra ⏱️ 10 Mins

Khakhra Nachos

Tex-Mex meets Gujarati heritage! Crisp plain khakhra triangles served with black beans, sour cream, and mango salsa.

Key Ingredients: Plain Khakhra, beans, sour cream, salsa, jalapeño slices.
  1. Break Plain Khakhras into triangular chip shapes.
  2. Arrange them in a circular pattern on a microwave-safe platter.
  3. Layer with seasoned boiled black beans and shredded cheese.
  4. Heat for 45 seconds to melt cheese. Top with cold sour cream and salsa.
Uses: Ratlami Sev ⏱️ 8 Mins

Ratlami Sev Puri

Classic flat street puris layered with standard potatoes, sweet garlic sauces, and overloaded with fiery Ratlami Sev.

Key Ingredients: Flat papdis, potato mash, garlic chutney, Ratlami Sev, raw mango slices.
  1. Arrange flat papdis on a plate.
  2. Place a small dollop of mashed spiced potato on each.
  3. Drizzle red chili-garlic chutney and green mint sauce.
  4. Cover the entire assembly with a mountain of coarse Ratlami Sev. Garnish with sour mango bits.
Uses: Mix Namkeen ⏱️ 5 Mins

Pantry Chavanu Bhel

Sweet, sour, and crunchy bhel made by tossing puffed rice with raw tomatoes, lemon, and sweet-sour Chavanu.

Key Ingredients: Mix Namkeen, puffed rice (kurmura), chopped tomato, onion, lemon juice.
  1. Mix equal parts puffed rice and Sonalben Mix Namkeen in a large bowl.
  2. Add chopped tomatoes, raw onions, and chopped coriander leaves.
  3. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of raw green mango syrup and fresh lemon juice.
  4. Toss together rapidly and serve in paper cones for the authentic street experience.
Uses: Fresh Thepla ⏱️ 5 Mins

Thepla Veggie Wrap

The ultimate travel wrap. Spread sweet mango pickle inside soft fresh methi theplas and roll with raw cucumber slices.

Key Ingredients: Thepla, mango pickle, cucumber strips, mint leaves.
  1. Warm up the methi thepla slightly on a pan.
  2. Spread a thin layer of Sonalben Spicy Mango Pickle gravy.
  3. Place fresh cucumber sticks and fresh mint leaves in the center.
  4. Roll up tightly in a foil wrapper. Perfect for lunch boxes and road trips.
Uses: Methi Khakhra ⏱️ 10 Mins

Cheese Khakhra Pizza

Crunchy whole wheat pizza. Methi khakhra base topped with tomato reduction, sweet corn, paneer, and lots of mozzarella.

Key Ingredients: Methi Khakhra, pizza reduction, sweet corn, paneer, grated mozzarella.
  1. Take one crisp Methi Khakhra.
  2. Brush lightly with olive oil or butter, then apply a layer of pizza sauce.
  3. Top with sweet corn kernels, diced bell peppers, and paneer cubes.
  4. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese and cook on a low pan covered with a lid until cheese melts.
Uses: Papad & Sev ⏱️ 10 Mins

Deconstructed Papdi Chaat

Flat besan papdi layered with sweetened yogurt, green chili chutney, boiled potatoes, and coarse papad sprinkles.

Key Ingredients: Flat papdis, sweet curd, potatoes, raw mango, spicy sev.
  1. Arrange papdi biscuits flat on a plate.
  2. Top each with mashed potatoes and chilled sweetened yogurt.
  3. Drizzle green chili and sweet date chutneys.
  4. Garnish with raw mango pieces, chili powder, and fine sev.
Uses: Gathiya & Sev ⏱️ 5 Mins

Instant Farsan Chaat

A quick dry mixture. Toss Bhavnagari Gathiya and plain sev with roasted peanuts, rock salt, coriander, and fresh lemon.

Key Ingredients: Gathiya, plain sev, peanuts, chopped coriander, lemon juice.
  1. Mix 1 cup Gathiya and 1/2 cup plain sev in a bowl.
  2. Toss in dry roasted peanuts and fine chopped green chilies.
  3. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the mixture and toss.
  4. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve immediately with hot ginger chai.
Uses: All Pantry ⏱️ 5 Mins

Traditional Tea Platters

Our suggestions for organizing the ultimate Gujarati high-tea. Arranging combinations of sweet and savory snacks.

Key Ingredients: Methi Khakhra, Gathiya, Jalebi, hot ginger tea.
  1. Prepare a classic cup of Masala Ginger Chai.
  2. Arrange 4 slices of Methi Khakhra on the left of a wooden platter.
  3. Fill a small bowl with Bhavnagari Gathiya and fried green chilies.
  4. Place a few fresh jalebis in the center to balance the savory bite with sweet syrup.
Uses: Jeera Khakhra ⏱️ 5 Mins

Travel Snack Packs

Pack mess-free Jeera Khakhra sandwiches wrapped in butter paper, accompanied by dry salted peanuts.

Key Ingredients: Jeera Khakhra, butter, dry peanuts, mango pickle pouch.
  1. Butter the inner faces of two Jeera Khakhras lightly.
  2. Press them together to form a dry, mess-free sandwich.
  3. Wrap securely in butter paper to prevent any breakages.
  4. Pack with single-serve pouches of mango pickle for easy dipping on train or car trips.
Uses: Diet Khakhra ⏱️ 8 Mins

Kids Tiffin Combos

Assemble a healthy lunch box with crumbled diet khakhra bowls, sweet raisins, and a small container of mango jam.

Key Ingredients: Diet Khakhra, raisins, fresh jam or curd, sweet makhana.
  1. Break Diet Khakhras into small, bite-sized crackers.
  2. Pack alongside a small spill-proof cup of sweet yogurt or fruit jam.
  3. Fill the second compartment with golden raisins and roasted sweet makhana.
  4. Provides a dry, clean, healthy, and high-fiber recess treat for school kids.
